## What is Tor Browser?
Tor Browser is a free, open-source tool designed for anonymous web browsing. It routes your internet traffic through a network of volunteer-operated servers, known as the Onion Router (Tor), to hide your IP address and location. Launched in 2002 by the Tor Project, it has evolved into a essential privacy tool for journalists, activists, and everyday users seeking protection from surveillance.

In 2026, Tor Browser version 13.x supports enhanced features like automatic updates, improved fingerprinting resistance, and better integration with HTTPS Everywhere. It's available for Windows, macOS, Linux, Android, and iOS, making it accessible worldwide.

### Why Use Tor for Privacy?
- **Anonymity**: Masks your online identity from websites and ISPs.
- **Censorship Bypass**: Access blocked content in restrictive regions.
- **Security**: Encrypts data multiple times, reducing eavesdropping risks.

However, Tor isn't foolproof—poor configuration or user errors can compromise anonymity.

## Understanding the dark&#12288;net
The dark&#12288;net refers to overlay networks accessible only through specialized software like Tor. Unlike the surface web (e.g., Google-searchable sites), dark&#12288;net sites use .onion domains and require Tor to connect. It emerged from early anonymity projects and now hosts a mix of legitimate and illicit content.

In 2026, the dark&#12288;net includes forums for whistleblowers, privacy-focused communities, and unfortunately, marketplaces for illegal goods. Estimates suggest it comprises less than 0.01% of the total internet but attracts significant attention due to its unregulated nature.
